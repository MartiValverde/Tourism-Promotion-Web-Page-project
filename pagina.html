About Riudellots

Riudellots is the perfect quiet getaway for people looking to flee the city's bustling sound. It can also be the perfect weekend plan, whether it's to enjoy it's nature or it's atmosphere.

Riudellots' name in Catalan means "Muddy River", this is in reference to the fact that in 1964 the town was flooded with 4m of mud after the river overflowed. What was it called beforehand you ask? Only god knows.

The town is located in the province of Girona, in Catalonia, Spain. It is located 12km away from Girona's capital and 92km from Barcelona. It has a population of around 4,000 people and an area of 13.1 km².

Riudellots also enjoys a variaety of local restaurants, with the main attractions being "Pizzeria 33" & "Via Augusta".
*Note that "Pizzeria 33" is closed on weekdays*

Notable Activities

  • FIPORC: "Fiporc" is Riudellots' main festival, since Riudellots is Europe's third town with the highest number of slaughtered each year, they celebrate the porcessing of pork.

    Enjoy many activities as well as incredible food cooked by farmers on the spot in "Fiporc"
    *Fiporc is usally helf in mid february, but it can vary from year to year*


















  • Walking: If what yu are looking for is a quiet walk in nature, Riudellots has many trails that you can take to enjoy the beautiful Catalan countryside.

    You can enjoy paths that go through the forest, or perhaps ones that follow one of the many rvers present in the procimity of Riudellots.

    There are also paths that go through the fields, where you can see farmers working and also animals grazing.





















  • Fundació Mona: If you are an animal lover, Fundació Mona is the place for you.

    Fundació Mona is a sanctuary for primates that have been rescued from illegal trafficking or from inadequate living conditions in captivity.

    You can visit the sanctuary and learn about the primates and the work that Fundació Mona does to protect them.
    *Visits must be booked in advance*
